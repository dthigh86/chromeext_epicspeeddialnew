True, free private search on a large scale is impossible to offer at present. We have tried for years now to obtain another private search partnership since Google annulled ours, but it simply isn’t possible. It’s cost us hundreds of thousands of dollars (even with the donations from thousands of you) over the past few years to keep Epic going. The expense is so great at our scale we couldn’t afford to launch our mobile browsers even though they are ready and fantastic. Epic everyday protects hundreds of thousands of people around the world. Our encrypted proxy/VPN is powered by hundreds of servers in eight different countries. To keep Epic an ongoing project, we had to become sustainable via our search options. Epic will now have two search options: free search by Yahoo & paid private search via EpicSearch.in.
When you use Yahoo search in Epic, you’ll get better search results and support our mission including more frequent releases and hundreds more servers for our encrypted proxy/VPN. According to their requirements, Yahoo search does bypass both our proxy and adblock. Upon ad click inYahoo search, the proxy and adblock remain disabled for several seconds. Their goal is to insure the integrity of their search ad marketplace. Due to their policies, a few other Yahoo sites including Techcrunch, Engadget, Autoblog, Huffington Post and AOL bypass our adblock. No other sites bypass our adblock or proxy so Epic works almost entirely as it always has except in respect to search and the other Yahoo sites. We will continue with Yahoo to work to offer free private search as we have been trying for years now.
We believe it is impossible at present to offer honest, free private search. We‘ve received many requests to support so-called private search engines like Startpage, DuckDuckGo and others. They refuse to explain to us how they work so we are unable to trust them. To our knowledge there are no exceptions to Google/Bing mandates to share the user’s IP address both to retrieve search ads and upon search ad click. It is misleading to claim to be private if you’re sharing your users’ data with Google/Bing. Why hide how you really work or mislead your users? Even those small search engines earn tens of millions of dollars a year. We’re nearly alone in the privacy software industry in our committment to transparency, honesty and privacy.
EpicSearch sends only your search query to a third-party to retrieve search results, never any IP or other personal information. It continues to be the only reasonably true, private, search engine in the world. To sustain it and keep it ad-free, we will soon charge a nominal $2 a month fee. All Epic users can use built-in Yahoo search for free.

We’re thrilled to now be on a sustainable path to soon serve millions of users everyday. Privacy is serious and it matters. This is Epic!
